French Press & Climate Urgency

Exploring rhetoric, tone, and framing across the political spectrum

Central Question

How do French media across the political spectrum construct the notion of climate urgency through their rhetoric, tone, and economic framing?

Hypotheses

H1 – Tone varies by political orientation: Left-leaning media are expected to adopt a more urgent and alarmist tone, center media a neutral and factual tone, and right-leaning media a tone that downplays or relativizes climate issues.

H2 – Emphasis on climate issues depends on political orientation: Left-leaning media focus on collective responsibility, inequalities, and climate damages; center media balance climate and economic concerns; right-leaning media highlight economic costs and the constraints of green laws.
